MSDS of Ethyl Acetate

Ethyl Acetate (EAC) - CAS No. 141-78-6

Trade Name
Ethyl Acetate (EAC)
Material Uses
Solvent for coating, organic synthesis, polyurethane systems and pharmaceutical Industry.
Supplier
Asia Pacific Petrochemical Co., Ltd.

Signal Word: Warning

GHS Classification
  • Flammable liquids: Category 2
  • Eye irritation: Category 2
  • Specific target organ toxicity: Category 3
Hazard Statements
  • H225: Highly flammable liquid and vapour.
  • H319: Causes serious eye irritation.
  • H336: May cause drowsiness or dizziness.

Composition

Chemical Name: Ethyl ethanoate, CAS No: 141-78-6, UN No: 1173

First-Aid Measures
  • Inhalation: Remove to fresh air. If breathing is difficult, give oxygen.
  • Skin Contact: Immediately flush skin with large amounts of water.
  • Eye Contact: Immediately flush eyes with large amounts of water for at least 10 minutes.
  • Ingestion: Do not induce vomiting. Transport to nearest medical facility.

Fire-Fighting Measures

Suitable Extinguishing Media: Water spray, dry chemical powder, alcohol-resistant foam, carbon dioxide.

Accidental Release Measures

Avoid contact with spilled material. Shut off leaks if possible. Remove all sources of ignition. Prevent from spreading or entering drains.

Handling
  • Avoid contact with skin, eyes, and clothing. Do not breathe vapours.
  • Keep away from heat, sparks, and open flames. No smoking.
  • Ground and bond all containers and receiving equipment to prevent static discharge.
  • Use only non-sparking tools and explosion-proof equipment.
  • Handle and open containers with care in a well-ventilated area.
Storage
  • Store in a cool, dry, and well-ventilated area away from sunlight and ignition sources.
  • Keep containers tightly closed when not in use.
  • Store locked up and away from incompatible materials like oxidizing agents, strong acids, and strong alkalis.
  • Bulk storage tanks should be diked (bunded).

Engineering Controls

Provide exhaust ventilation or other engineering controls to keep the airborne concentrations of vapours below their respective threshold limit value.

Personal Protective Equipment (PPE)
  • Eye/Face Protection: Use chemical splash goggles or a full face shield where splashing is possible.
  • Hand Protection: Wear impervious protective gloves (e.g., Butyl rubber, Neoprene).
  • Respiratory Protection: Use an approved vapor respirator if ventilation is inadequate or exposure limits are exceeded.
  • Body Protection: Wear protective clothing which is chemical resistant to this material. Safety shoes and boots should also be chemical resistant.
